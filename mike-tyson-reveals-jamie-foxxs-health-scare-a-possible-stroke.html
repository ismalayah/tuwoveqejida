Mike Tyson Reveals Jamie Foxxs Health Scare: A Possible Stroke

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Sunday, July 21, 2024

Mike Tyson, the boxing icon, has disclosed that actor Jamie Foxx experienced a potential stroke, following earlier reports of a mysterious health scare last month.

The 55-year-old actor has been receiving treatment at a specialized facility in Chicago, catering to adults with severe, complex conditions, including stroke recovery.

During an appearance on the Valuetainment podcast, former boxer Mike mentioned Jamie’s health, stating, ‘He’s not feeling well. They said a stroke. I have no idea what happened to him.’

Jamie, born Eric Bishop, was filming the action-comedy Back In Action alongside Cameron Diaz in Atlanta, Georgia, in April when he was hospitalized.

Reports suggest that Jamie’s health scare has disrupted the filming, with 51-year-old Cameron resorting to using body doubles in his absence.

Jamie’s daughter Corinne visited him at the rehabilitation center over the weekend.

Corinne was seen arriving at the facility, renowned for its top-notch rehabilitation services, specializing in the treatment of severe conditions like strokes, traumatic brain injuries, and spinal cord injuries.


On May 12, Corinne provided an update on Jamie’s condition, assuring that her father had been discharged from the hospital for weeks and is receiving excellent care, already on the road to recovery.

According to Corinne, Jamie arrived at the facility in late April and is making good progress after initially receiving treatment at an Atlanta hospital.

Shortly after Jamie’s supposed stroke, sources revealed to Page Six that he suffers from high blood pressure and was luckily able to survive the health scare.
